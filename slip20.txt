Q1. What legal provisions under the Indian IT Act can be used to seek compensation for damages caused by cyber defamation?
The  may apply where cyber defamation involves unauthorized access, misuse of accounts, identity theft, or unlawful publication through digital means.

Section 43 – Compensation for unauthorized access/damage to computer systems where relevant.

Section 66C – Identity theft if fake profiles/accounts are used.

Section 66D – Cheating by personation through digital platforms.

Defamation compensation/damages may also be pursued through civil remedies and criminal defamation laws outside the IT Act.

Electronic evidence such as screenshots, URLs, and chat logs can support claims.

Q2. How can the Indian IT Act, 2000 be used to prosecute someone involved in cyberbullying?
File a complaint with cyber police/cyber crime portal.

Preserve digital evidence—screenshots, chats, emails, account details.

Use relevant provisions of the  depending on conduct, such as:

Section 66C – Identity theft

Section 66D – Cheating by personation

Section 67 – Publishing/transmitting prohibited obscene material (where applicable)

Authorities can trace offenders using digital evidence/IP logs.

Offenders may face imprisonment, fines, or both upon conviction.
