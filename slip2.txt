Social engineering is a technique used by attackers to manipulate employees into revealing confidential information.



1. Awareness programs about phishing, vishing, and baiting
2. Conduct phishing simulation tests
3. Enforce strong password & MFA policies
4. Identity verification before sharing data
5. Incident reporting mechanism
6. Data protection and confidentiality training
7. Regular updates on latest threats
8. Role-based training for sensitive positions


Employee awareness reduces human errors and prevents fraud.


The **Information Technology Act, 2000** provides legal support:


 Defines cybercrimes (Sec 43, 66, 66C, 66D)
 Enables digital evidence collection
 Provides punishment (jail + fine)
 Helps victims claim compensation
 Empowers law enforcement



It ensures legal action against cybercriminals.
