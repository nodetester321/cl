Q1. How can social media platforms be held accountable under the Indian IT Act for hosting defamatory content?
Platforms may lose intermediary protection if they fail to act after receiving lawful notice of illegal/defamatory content.

They must comply with due diligence obligations under Indian intermediary rules.

Authorities/courts can direct removal or blocking of unlawful content.

Failure to cooperate with lawful investigations may attract penalties.

Victims can seek court orders requiring takedown and disclosure of offender details where legally permitted.

Liability may arise if the platform actively participates in creation/promotion of unlawful content.

Q2. How can online platforms be held liable for cyberbullying incidents under Indian cyber law?
Platforms must provide reporting/grievance mechanisms for harmful content.

They may be required to remove unlawful cyberbullying content after valid notice/order.

Failure to follow due diligence requirements can affect safe-harbor protections.

Platforms may need to preserve evidence and cooperate with law enforcement.

Repeated negligence in handling abuse complaints can increase legal/regulatory scrutiny.

Victims may seek takedown orders and legal remedies through authorities/courts.
