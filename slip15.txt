Q1. Report on “Online Piracy and Copyright Infringement: A Case Study of Illegal Downloading and Streaming”
Introduction:
Online piracy refers to unauthorized downloading, copying, or streaming of copyrighted digital content.

Case Study:

A website illegally uploads movies/software without permission.

Users download/stream the content for free.

Copyright owners file complaints against the platform.

Legal Issues:

Violates Indian copyright laws and digital content regulations.

May involve cyber law provisions when digital systems are used for unlawful distribution.

Website operators can face penalties, fines, and legal action.

Impact:

Financial loss to creators/companies

Reduced incentive for innovation/content creation

Growth of illegal digital marketplaces

Conclusion:
Online piracy is a punishable offence that harms creators and businesses.

Q2. Steps organizations can take to protect user privacy when collecting data for analysis
Obtain clear and informed user consent before data collection.

Collect only necessary/minimum data.

Publish transparent privacy policies.

Encrypt stored and transmitted data.

Restrict access to authorized personnel only.

Anonymize/pseudonymize data used for analysis where possible.

Conduct regular security audits and risk assessments.

Train employees on privacy and data handling practices.

Establish data retention and deletion policies.

Comply with applicable privacy and cyber laws.
